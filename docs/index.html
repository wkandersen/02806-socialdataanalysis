Socioeconomic Indicators in Chicago

Lets take a moment to look at the socioeconomic state of Chicago. To measure this, we will look at the Census Data (CityOfChicago.org) collected by the city of Chicago. This dataset contains a variety of socioeconomic indicators for each of the 77 community areas in Chicago, including the Hardship Index, which is a composite measure of socioeconomic hardship based on factors such as unemployment, poverty, and education levels. The socioeconomic data provides a better understanding of how life is for the residents of Chicago, and how it may relate to crime patterns in the city.

Hardship across Chicago

This visualization shows the distribution of the Hardship Index across different community areas in Chicago. It clearly shows the areas with lowest hardship along the coast of Lake Michigan.

Per Capita Income across Chicago

The difference in income across the community areas of Chicago is clearly visible in this choropleth map of per capita income.
